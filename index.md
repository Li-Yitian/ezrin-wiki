# Ezrin（EZR，P15311）Protein Structure Wiki

## 1. Intoduction
Ezrin is a core member of the ERM protein family (Ezrin-Radixin-Moesin), a human peripheral membrane protein with a highly conserved modular structural architecture. It consists of linearly arranged functional domains with typical reversible intramolecular conformational changes, and all structural features are strictly conserved in its primary and tertiary structural levels.

## 2. Primary Structural Basis
Human Ezrin is encoded by a single polypeptide chain composed of 585 amino acid residues, with no disulfide bond formation in the primary structure. Its amino acid sequence is characterized by domain-specific conservation: the N-terminal and C-terminal regions show high sequence homology among ERM family members, while the central linker region has moderate sequence variability.

## 3. Core Modular Tertiary Structure
Ezrin’s tertiary structure forms three spatially distinct structural domains with unique structural characteristics, connected by flexible motifs, which is the most typical structural feature of the protein.

### (1) N-terminal FERM Domain
A ~300-residue globular domain folded by three tandem subdomains (F1, F2, F3). Each subdomain contains a conserved β-barrel core and variable surface loop regions; the whole FERM domain forms a compact clover-like 3D structure with a smooth hydrophobic surface and specific electrostatic interaction patches.
### (2) Central Linker Region
An unstructured flexible linker motif of ~40 amino acids, rich in proline residues. It has a linear polypeptide backbone with rigid-flexible coupling characteristics, serving as a structural bridge for spatial connection between the FERM domain and C-terminal domain without forming a stable secondary structure.
### (3) C-terminal Domain (CTD)
A ~240-residue helical bundle domain composed of multiple parallel/antiparallel α-helices. Its C-terminal segment contains a conserved extended α-helical motif, and the whole domain presents an elongated structural conformation in the free state with a hydrophobic patch on the surface.

## 4.Intramolecular Conformational States
Ezrin exists in two reversible native conformational statesdriven by intramolecular domain interactions, with no covalent modification involved in the basic structural transition, which is a core structural property of Ezrin.

### 4.1 Closed Conformation PDB: 4RM8
Closed Conformation: The CTD binds tightly to the F3 subdomain of the FERM domain via hydrophobic interactions and hydrogen bonds, masking the surface structural motifs of both domains and forming a compact globular overall structure.

### 4.2 Open ConformationPDB: 1NI2
Open Conformation: The intramolecular domain interaction is disrupted, leading to spatial separation of the FERM domain and CTD. Each domain restores its independent 3D structure, with all surface structural motifs fully exposed, forming an extended linear overall structure.
